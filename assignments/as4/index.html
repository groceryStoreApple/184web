Inverse Kinematics

This is the presentation of the results from the inverse kinematics rendering implemented by Siqi Zuo and me. Demo video at link

We used the framework from the last assignment to support useful utilities such as rotating the camera, zooming in and out, switching between wireframe mode and flat and smooth shading mode. Rotation and zoom are particularly conveninent to view the result. Press arrow keys to rotate. Press +/- to zoom in and out.

In the demo, we had two arms, each with five segments of varying lengths. The two yellow large balls are the objects that the arms follow. The ball that are further away from the center is moving in a figure eight trajectory. It sometimes goes out of reach of the arm. It was to demonstrate that the algorithm was able to remain stable and tries to reach the ball as much as possible. The ball that is closer to the center are oscillating along y-axis, it passes through the base point to demonstrate the stability of the solver

We implement the extra credit feature of multiple arms.
